History Of Women In Stem


Elizabeth Blackwell (1821-1910) -> The first woman in America to receive a medical degree, Elizabeth Blackwell championed the participation of women in the medical profession and ultimately opened her own medical college for women.


Lise Meitner (1878-1968) -> In mid-1938, Meitner with chemists Otto Hahn and Fritz Strassmann at the Kaiser Wilhelm Institute found that bombarding thorium with neutrons produced different isotopes.


Florence Seibert (1897-1991) -> n American biochemist who invented the first reliable tuberculosis test and helped improve the safety of intravenous injections. Born in Easton, Pennsylvania, Seibert contracted polio at an early age.


Cecilia Payne (1900-1979) -> A British-born American astronomer and astrophysicist who proposed in her 1925 doctoral thesis that stars were composed primarily of hydrogen and helium.


Grace Hopper (1906-1992) -> An American computer scientist and United States Navy rear admiral. One of the first programmers of the Harvard Mark I computer, she was a pioneer of computer programming who invented one of the first linkers.
